Telescope Criteria Calculators

Specifying a telescope is a matter of compromise. Greater aperture results in restricted field of view; increasing the focal ratio increases the telescope's length; and the eye's pupil must always be taken into account.

We are familiar with the standard relationships such as:

telescope focal length = focal ratio * mirror diameter, and
focal ratio = eyepiece focal length / eye pupil

Is there a master relationship that allows us to compare essential telescope specifications? And what is the minimum set of criteria?

The master equation should involve our eye; the eyepiece; and the telescope's aperture and true field of view. Doing so results in two master relationships. The second relationship substitutes focal ratio for both the eyepiece's focal length and our eye's pupil:

1) aperture * true field of view * eyepiece focal length = eyepiece field stop * eye pupil
    - or -
2) aperture * true field of view * focal ratio = eyepiece field stop

Note that the first relationship involves five criteria and the second relationship just four criteria.

Specifically, the five telescope design criteria are:

  • the eye's pupil diameter
  • the eyepiece's focal length
  • the eyepiece's field stop diameter
  • the telescope's aperture
  • the telescope's real field of view
Set any four of the criteria to compute the fifth.
